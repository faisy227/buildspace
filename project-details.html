Project Overview

The Cliffside Residence is a 4,200 square foot private home located on a dramatic coastal site in Big Sur, California. The project was completed in 2022 after a three-year design and construction process.Â 

The clients, a couple relocating from San Francisco, sought a retreat that would connect them with nature while providing all the comforts of modern living. Their brief emphasized sustainability, seamless indoor-outdoor living, and a design that would respect the site’s natural beauty.

Design Concept

The design concept for the Cliffside Residence emerged from careful study of the site’s topography, microclimate, and views. The home is organized as a series of interconnected pavilions that follow the natural contours of the land, minimizing excavation and preserving existing vegetation.

Key design elements include:

  • Cantilevered forms that appear to float above the landscape
  • A central courtyard that provides shelter from coastal winds
  • Floor-to-ceiling glass walls that maximize ocean views
  • Natural materials that blend with the surroundings
  • Strategic window placement for passive solar heating

The interior spaces flow seamlessly into outdoor living areas, with sliding glass walls that completely disappear to merge indoors and out. The material palette was carefully selected to complement the natural surroundings, with locally sourced stone, reclaimed wood, and weathering steel that develops a protective patina over time.

Material & Finishes

The material selection for the Cliffside Residence was driven by sustainability, durability, and aesthetic harmony with the natural surroundings. We prioritized locally sourced materials and those with low embodied energy.

Reclaimed Redwood

Sourced from deconstructed Northern California barns, used for exterior siding and interior feature walls.

Local Sandstone

Quarried within 50 miles of the site, used for foundation walls and interior flooring in common areas.

Weathering Steel

Used for structural elements and exterior cladding, developing a protective rust patina over time.

Sustainability Features

The Cliffside Residence was designed to achieve LEED Gold certification and operates as a net-zero energy home. Our sustainability strategy addressed energy efficiency, water conservation, material selection, and indoor environmental quality.

Solar Energy

18kW photovoltaic array meets 100% of the home’s energy needs

Water Savings

40% reduction in water use through low-flow fixtures and rainwater harvesting

Recycled Content

Over 30% of construction materials contain recycled content

Local Materials

75% of materials sourced within 500 miles of the site

Energy Efficency

50% more efficient than California’s strict energy code

Low-VOC

All paints, adhesives, and finishes meet strict VOC limits

Design Process

The design process for the Cliffside Residence was highly collaborative, involving the clients, architect, builder, and various consultants from the earliest stages. This integrated approach ensured that all aspects of the design – from site planning to material selection – worked together seamlessly.

1

Site Analysis

Comprehensive study of topography, views, solar orientation, wind patterns, and existing vegetation to inform the design.

2

Concept Development

Creation of multiple design concepts exploring different approaches to siting, massing, and spatial organization.

3

Design Development

Refinement of the selected concept, with detailed studies of materials, structural systems, and building systems.

4

Construction Documents

Preparation of detailed drawings and specifications to communicate the design intent to the builder.

5

Construction Administration

Ongoing collaboration with the builder during construction to ensure design integrity.
